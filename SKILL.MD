## Mandatory Research-First Workflow

**This is not optional. Run every step before writing.**

### Step 1 — Define Intent
Answer before searching:
- Who is reading this? (dental clinic owner, practice manager, principal dentist)
- What do they do after reading? (book a call, fix their GBP, change a process)
- What keyword or suburb is this targeting?
- Is this for blog only, Facebook only, or both? (default = both)

### Step 2 — Web Search (run all 4 searches)

```
Search 1 — Current news/updates on the topic:
  "[topic] australia 2025" OR "2026"

Search 2 — AU industry sources:
  site:ahpra.gov.au [topic]
  site:ada.org.au [topic]
  "[topic]" dental australia

Search 3 — Competitor content gap:
  [target keyword] → read top 3 results:
    What angle do they take?
    What do they miss?
    How old is the content?
    Do they reference regional Victoria or Bendigo? (almost never — that's our gap)

Search 4 — Bendigo/regional Victoria specific (always run this):
  "dental clinic Bendigo" OR "dentist Bendigo" [topic]
  "regional victoria dental" [topic]
  This surfaces locally-relevant angles competitors never cover.
```

### Step 3 — Source Quality Check

| Tier | Examples | Use? |
|------|----------|------|
| ✅ Tier 1 — cite directly | ahpra.gov.au, ada.org.au, health.gov.au, acma.gov.au, legislation.gov.au | Always |
| ✅ Tier 2 — reference with attribution | ABC Health, The Medical Republic, Pulse+IT, ADA news, Healthdirect, Australian Dental Journal | Yes, with link |
| ⚠️ Tier 3 — use carefully | General SEO blogs, agency content | Only if no AU source exists |
| ❌ Never use | US stats as AU, unattributed claims, content 2+ years old on fast-moving topics | Never |

**Minimum: 2 Tier 1 or Tier 2 AU sources per piece.**

## Blog Post Formula

### Structure (every post, every time)

```
META TITLE       — keyword-led, ≤60 chars, includes "Australia" or state/suburb where natural
META DESCRIPTION — ≤160 chars, keyword + clear benefit + soft CTA

H1               — matches or closely mirrors meta title
INTRO (2–3 para) — hook with the "news angle" from research; establish why this matters
                   for a Bendigo or regional Vic clinic owner specifically

H2 — [First major point, ideally a named recent change or finding]
  Body: 2–3 paragraphs. Source cited inline. Bendigo/regional angle present.

H2 — [Second major point]
  Body: same approach.

H2 — [Third major point]
  Body: same approach.

H2 — What [Suburb/Regional Vic] Clinic Owners Should Do This Week
  3–5 specific, actionable steps. Numbered. Plain language.

OUTRO + CTA
  1 soft-close paragraph.
  "Want to know how this applies to your [Bendigo/Ballarat/regional Vic] clinic?
   Book a free 20-minute call → [calendly.com/quadgrowth]"

BYLINE
  "QuadGrowth is a Bendigo-based AI-powered marketing agency for healthcare clinics
   across Victoria. quadgrowth.com.au"
```

### On-Page SEO Checklist (confirm before finalising)

- [ ] Primary keyword in H1
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] Primary keyword in meta title and description
- [ ] "Bendigo" or regional suburb named within first 200 words
- [ ] At least 2 H2s contain related or secondary keywords
- [ ] Internal link → nearest relevant service or suburb page on quadgrowth.com.au
- [ ] External link → Tier 1 or Tier 2 AU source
- [ ] URL slug clean and keyword-rich: `/blog/[keyword-hyphenated]`
- [ ] All factual claims traced to a named source from Step 2
- [ ] AHPRA compliance confirmed — no health outcome claims, no unsubstantiated stats
- [ ] Australian English throughout (optimise, colour, practise, organise, etc.)

### Word Count Targets

| Post type | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Standard blog post | 900–1,400 words |
| Pillar / cornerstone page | 2,000–3,000 words |
| Quick update / news post | 500–700 words |

## Facebook Post Formula

Every blog gets a Facebook version. Also write standalone Facebook posts for major AU dental/health news.

### The Formula

```
LINE 1 — HOOK
  One punchy, specific, surprising line. Stop the scroll.
  Never start with "QuadGrowth" or "We".
  Start with a fact, a number, a question, or a provocation.
  Make it regionally grounded where possible.
  e.g. "Most dental clinics in Bendigo don't appear in Google's AI results — here's why."

LINES 2–4 — THE SUBSTANCE
  2–3 short paragraphs. One idea each.
  Name the source. Be specific. Give genuine insight.
  Include the Bendigo/regional Victoria angle.

LINE 5 — THE BRIDGE
  Connect the finding to their practice.
  "Here's what this means for your Bendigo clinic..."
  "This is exactly why regional practices need to act on this now..."

LINE 6 — THE CTA
  Low friction. One action only.
  "Free 20-min clinic audit → calendly.com/quadgrowth"
  OR "Drop a 🦷 if you've run into this."

HASHTAGS (3–5 max)
  Always include: #QuadGrowth #BendigoBusiness
  Rotate: #DentalMarketing #RegionalVictoria #BendigoDentist
           #VictoriaDental #HealthcareMarketing #BallartDental
           #SheppartonBusiness #GeelongDental

NOTE: Put source URLs in the first comment — not in the post body.
Facebook penalises posts with external links in the body text.
```

## AHPRA Quick Rules (apply to every piece)

| ❌ Never write | ✅ Write instead |
|----------------|-----------------|
| "Guaranteed results" | "Clinics we work with have seen growth in bookings" |
| "Get X new patients" | "More new patient enquiries each month" |
| "Best agency in Bendigo" | "A Bendigo-based dental marketing agency" |
| Testimonials with health outcomes | Comments about service/experience only |
| Unsourced statistics | Named source + stat |
| "Your patients will get better care" | Never — we don't make clinical claims |

When any AHPRA risk is detected → flag it and hand off to `au-legal-compliance-agent`.
